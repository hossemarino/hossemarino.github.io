About

I am a research scholar at Queen's University of Belfast with a focus on urban planning, religious studies, and the intersection of neoliberalism with contemporary society. My academic work explores the relationship between religious institutions, urban spaces, and economic policies.

My research is supported by the Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC) and investigates the complex nexus between religion, planning policy, and social change. I am committed to producing rigorous, impactful research that contributes meaningfully to understanding contemporary urban and social challenges.

Publications & Written Works

Religion-Planning Nexus: Neoliberalism, 'Blind Spots' and Places of Worship

Research Project (ESRC Funded)

Queen's University of Belfast | 2022-2026

This research project examines the complex relationship between neoliberal economic policies, urban planning decisions, and their impact on religious institutions and places of worship. The project identifies policy 'blind spots' where current planning frameworks may inadequately address the needs and challenges of faith communities.
